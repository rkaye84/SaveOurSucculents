If your orchid appears to become unhappy (stop blooming), replotting may be necessary. This should be done every other year anyway, as orchids can outgrow their homes or the soil may become crumbly. To replot, pick a slightly larger pot (orchid containers have holes to increase air circulation). Put your orchid potting mix into a large bowl and cover with boiling water. Drain once it reaches room temperature. They are very sensitive when it comes to bacteria and germs. Make a solution of Â½ cup of household bleach and 1 gallon of water. Soak the planter in this, as well as any tools you use. Wash your hands before you proceed. Gently pull the pot away from the plant and wash off the roots. Use sharp scissors to cut off any brown or rotting roots. Fill the new planter with the soaked potting medium and place the plant so that the base is right at the top of the medium. Use a chopstick to help push bits of planting medium in between the roots. Keep the orchid misted for at least a week until the new roots begin to appear.
